\section*{Linear regression}

Response variable $Y$ is log of half-life for uranium ($Z=90$)
and thorium ($Z=88$).
Note: $Z$ is atomic number of daughter nucleus.
\begin{equation*}
\begin{matrix}
Y & A & Z & E
\\[1ex]
6.30 & 228 & 90 & 6.80
\\
14.4 & 230 & 90 & 5.99
\\
21.5 & 232 & 90 & 5.41
\\
29.7 & 234 & 90 & 4.86
\\
34.2 & 236 & 90 & 4.57
\\
39.5 & 238 & 90 & 4.27
\\
7.52 & 226 & 88 & 6.45
\\
17.9 & 228 & 88 & 5.52
\\
28.5 & 230 & 88 & 4.77
\\
40.6 & 232 & 88 & 4.08
\end{matrix}
\end{equation*}

Geiger–Nuttall regression model.
\begin{equation*}
\hat Y=\beta_2\frac{Z}{\sqrt E}+\beta_1Z^{2/3}+\beta_0
\end{equation*}

By ordinary least squares the best coefficients for the above data are
\begin{align*}
\beta_2&=3.7
\\
\beta_1&=-3.058
\\
\beta_0&=-60.14
\end{align*}

\begin{verbatim}
X = zero(3,10)

X[2] = Z^(2/3)
X[3] = X[3] + 1

beta = dot(inv(dot(X,transpose(X))),X,Y)

Yhat = dot(beta,X)
\end{verbatim}
